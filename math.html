Math Grade 4 - 12

Unlocking the Power of Numbers.

Topics We Cover

Grade 4

  • Addition and subtraction of multi-digit numbers.
  • Multiplication and division of whole numbers.
  • Fractions: equivalent fractions, comparing fractions, adding and subtracting fractions with like denominators.
  • Decimals: decimal place value, addition, subtraction, and basic operations.
  • Geometry: basic shapes, angles, symmetry, and area and perimeter.
  • Measurement: customary and metric units, time, and money.
  • Data and statistics: collecting and interpreting data, basic graphs and charts.

Grade 5

  • Whole number operations (continued)
  • Fractions and decimals (continued)
  • Patterns and relationships
  • Geometry: classifying shapes, volume, and graphing on the coordinate plane
  • Measurement conversions
  • Introduction to basic algebraic concepts
  • Introduction to probability and statistics

Grade 6 - 8

  • Ratios and proportions
  • Percents and their applications
  • Integers and rational numbers
  • Expressions, equations, and inequalities
  • Coordinate geometry
  • Basic concepts of geometry: angles, congruence, similarity, and transformations
  • Data analysis, probability, and statistics (more in-depth)
  • Functions and relations
  • Introduction to linear algebra and systems of equations

Grade 9

  • Advanced algebra: quadratic equations, factoring, and polynomials
  • Linear equations and inequalities
  • Exponents and radicals
  • Functions and their properties
  • Introduction to trigonometry
  • Geometry: advanced concepts such as circles, triangles, and quadrilaterals

Grade 10

  • More advanced algebra: rational expressions, exponential functions, logarithms
  • Trigonometry (continued): trigonometric functions and identities
  • Coordinate geometry (advanced)
  • Geometry: 3D geometry and conic sections (circles, ellipses, parabolas, hyperbolas)

Grade 11 - 12

  • Calculus: differential calculus (limits, derivatives) and integral calculus (antiderivatives, integrals)
  • Advanced statistics and probability
  • Discrete mathematics: combinatorics, graph theory, and number theory
  • Linear algebra: matrix operations, determinants, and vectors
  • Advanced algebra and functions
  • Advanced trigonometry and complex numbers
  • Mathematical modeling and problem-solving
  • Optional specialized topics like advanced calculus, advanced statistics, or advanced geometry depending on the curriculum and student interests.
